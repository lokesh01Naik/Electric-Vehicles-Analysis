Electric Vehicle Data Analysis Dashboard 
Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing Electric Vehicle (EV) adoption trends using data visualization and business intelligence tools. The analysis helps understand the growth, distribution, and key performance indicators (KPIs) of electric vehicles including Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs).

The goal of this project is to provide insights into EV market growth, manufacturer dominance, and geographical adoption patterns.

Problem Statement

The project aims to analyze the electric vehicle dataset and extract meaningful insights using different KPIs and visualizations.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Total Vehicles

Understand the total number of electric vehicles in the dataset including BEVs and PHEVs.

Average Electric Range

Calculate the average electric range to evaluate technological efficiency of EVs.

Total BEV Vehicles

Identify the total number of Battery Electric Vehicles.

Calculate the percentage of BEVs among all electric vehicles.

Total PHEV Vehicles

Identify the total number of Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles.

Calculate the percentage of PHEVs among all electric vehicles.

Dashboard Visualizations

The following visualizations are used to understand EV trends:

1️⃣ Total Vehicles by Model Year

Visualization: Line / Area Chart
Purpose: Shows EV growth trends from 2010 onwards.

2️⃣ Total Vehicles by State

Visualization: Map Chart
Purpose: Displays geographic distribution of electric vehicles.

3️⃣ Top 10 Vehicles by Make

Visualization: Bar Chart
Purpose: Identifies top EV manufacturers based on number of vehicles.

4️⃣ Total Vehicles by CAFV Eligibility

Visualization: Pie / Donut Chart
Purpose: Shows vehicles eligible for Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle incentives.

5️⃣ Top 10 Vehicles by Model

Visualization: Tree Map
Purpose: Highlights the most popular EV models.

Rapid growth in electric vehicle adoption after 2015.

Certain manufacturers dominate the EV market.

EV adoption varies significantly by region.

Government incentives (CAFV eligibility) influence EV adoption.
